BAKERY INGREDIENT SUPPLY AND REBATE AGREEMENT

This Bakery Ingredient Supply and Rebate Agreement (this "Agreement") is made
as of December 30, 2026, between Maria's Bakery LLC, a California limited
liability company ("Bakery"), and Valley Provisions LLC, a regional food
service supplier ("Supplier").

1. PRODUCTS

1.1 Products. Supplier shall sell to Bakery flour, butter, dairy products,
yeast, eggs, fillings, paper packaging, refrigerated ingredients, and related
bakery supplies (collectively, the "Products").

1.2 Product Standards. Products shall conform to the specifications,
temperature requirements, shelf-life requirements, labeling, and packaging
standards customarily used by Supplier for similarly situated bakery
customers.

1.3 New Products. Bakery may request specialty items. Supplier may accept or
reject specialty-item requests depending on availability, supplier capacity,
minimum order sizes, and mutually agreed pricing.

2. ACTIVATION OF MONTHLY MINIMUM

2.1 Dormant Period. This Agreement is available for ordering when signed, but
the monthly minimum purchase obligation is dormant until Bakery signs a
wholesale breakfast delivery contract requiring weekday deliveries.

2.2 Activation Event. Upon signing of such wholesale delivery contract,
Bakery's monthly purchase minimum under Section 3 activates automatically.

3. MONTHLY MINIMUM

3.1 Minimum Purchase. While active, Bakery shall purchase not less than
$4,500.00 of Products during each calendar month.

3.2 Measurement. Purchases are measured by invoice price, net of credits,
taxes, delivery charges separately stated, and disputed amounts not yet
resolved.

3.3 Failure to Meet Minimum. Failure to meet the monthly minimum does not
automatically terminate this Agreement, but Supplier may suspend preferred
pricing, reduce reserved inventory, or require revised forecasts if Bakery
misses the minimum for two consecutive months.

4. FORECASTS AND ORDERS

4.1 Forecasts. Bakery shall provide a rolling four-week forecast of expected
Product needs while a wholesale delivery route is active.

4.2 Weekly Orders. Bakery shall place weekly orders by Wednesday for Products
needed the following week unless the parties agree on another ordering
schedule.

4.3 Confirmation. Supplier shall confirm accepted orders by email or portal
notice. An order is binding only when accepted by Supplier.

5. VOLUME REBATE

5.1 Rebate Threshold. If Bakery purchases at least $8,000.00 of Products in
a calendar month, Supplier shall issue Bakery a $400.00 credit for that
month.

5.2 Rebate Timing. Supplier shall apply the rebate as a credit on the next
monthly statement unless the Agreement has ended, in which case Supplier shall
pay the rebate within thirty days after final reconciliation.

5.3 No Double Counting. Returned Products, credits, and non-Product charges
do not count toward the rebate threshold.

6. DELIVERY AND COLD CHAIN

6.1 Pickup Window. Supplier shall make refrigerated Products available for
pickup within a four-hour pickup window confirmed for the applicable order.

6.2 Temperature Control. Refrigerated Products shall be held in commercially
reasonable temperature-controlled conditions until pickup or delivery.

6.3 Cold-Chain Shortfall. A "Cold-Chain Shortfall" occurs if Supplier's
temperature-control failure or pickup-window failure materially prevents
Bakery from timely using Products for the wholesale breakfast route.

6.4 Cooperation. Upon a Cold-Chain Shortfall, Supplier shall cooperate in
substitute sourcing, expedited replacement, or reasonable order adjustment.

7. SPOILAGE AND RISK OF LOSS

7.1 Supplier Responsibility. Supplier is responsible for spoilage caused by
Supplier's failure to maintain required cold-chain conditions before pickup or
delivery.

7.2 Bakery Responsibility. Bakery is responsible for spoilage caused by
Bakery delay, vehicle failure, failure to maintain refrigeration after pickup,
or improper handling after risk of loss passes.

8. PRICE ADJUSTMENTS

Supplier may adjust prices for commodity changes, freight increases, labor
costs, packaging changes, or supplier cost increases, provided Supplier gives
Bakery reasonable advance notice before the adjusted price applies to future
orders.

9. PAYMENT

Bakery shall pay undisputed invoices according to Supplier's standard payment
terms. Bakery may dispute an invoice in good faith by written notice
describing the disputed amount and reason for dispute. Undisputed amounts
remain payable when due.

10. TERM AND TERMINATION

10.1 Term. This Agreement begins on the effective date and continues until
terminated.

10.2 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate on thirty days'
written notice.

10.3 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate for material breach
that remains uncured thirty days after written notice, except payment default
may be subject to a shorter cure period if stated in Supplier's credit terms.

11. CONFIDENTIALITY

Pricing, forecasts, customer route volume, recipes, formulas, and business
plans are confidential and may be used only to perform this Agreement.

12. MISCELLANEOUS

This Agreement may be amended only in writing. Neither party may assign this
Agreement without consent except in connection with a sale of substantially
all assets or a merger. California law governs.
