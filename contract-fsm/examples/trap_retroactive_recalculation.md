# MedFab–RegionalHealth Diagnostic Device Procurement Agreement
**Contract No. MF-RH-2025-03**
Effective Date: 1 March 2025
Between: MedFab Precision Manufacturing LLC ("Supplier") and RegionalHealth
Network ("Buyer")

---

## 1. Scope

Supplier shall manufacture, test, and deliver twenty (20) portable diagnostic
imaging units ("Units") to Buyer's regional facilities in accordance with the
Specifications (Exhibit A). Each Unit is identified by serial number U-01
through U-20.

Total fixed price: **$6,000,000** ($300,000 per Unit).

---

## 2. Delivery Schedule

All twenty (20) Units shall be delivered to Buyer's central receiving facility
(Austin Logistics Hub) no later than **PED + 210 days** (the "Required
Delivery Date"), where PED is the Purchase Effective Date. Supplier may
deliver Units in batches at its discretion.

For purposes of this Agreement, "delivered" means physical receipt at the
Austin Logistics Hub confirmed by Buyer's written receiving log.

---

## 3. Price Adjustments at Initial Delivery

### 3.1 Late Delivery Penalty

If any Unit is not delivered by the Required Delivery Date, Supplier shall pay
Buyer liquidated damages of **$1,500 per Unit per calendar day** of delay,
beginning the first calendar day after the Required Delivery Date. A grace
period of twenty-one (21) calendar days applies; no penalty accrues within the
grace period. After the grace period, penalty accrues back to day one of delay
(i.e., the grace period is a threshold, not an offset — if delay exceeds 21
days, all days from day 1 are penalised, not just days 22 onwards).

The per-Unit late delivery penalty shall not exceed **$90,000 per Unit**
($300,000 × 30%). The aggregate late delivery penalty across all twenty Units
shall not exceed **$1,200,000**.

### 3.2 Early Delivery Incentive

If a Unit is delivered **before** the Required Delivery Date, Buyer shall pay
Supplier **$750 per Unit per calendar day early**, calculated from the actual
delivery date to the Required Delivery Date. The maximum early delivery
incentive shall not exceed **$22,500 per Unit** ($300,000 × 7.5%). No
aggregate cap applies to early delivery incentives.

### 3.3 Initial Calculation is Provisional

**All penalty and incentive amounts calculated under Sections 3.1 and 3.2 at
the time of physical delivery are provisional and subject to revision** under
Section 4. No payment of provisional penalties or incentives is made at the
time of physical delivery. Settlement occurs only after the process in
Section 4 is complete for all Units.

---

## 4. Acceptance Testing and Retroactive Date Revision

### 4.1 Acceptance Testing Period

Following physical delivery of each Unit, Buyer shall conduct acceptance
testing within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt (the "Testing Window").
If Buyer does not issue a defect notice within the Testing Window, the Unit
is deemed accepted and the initial provisional calculations under Section 3
become final for that Unit.

### 4.2 First Defect Correction

If Buyer's acceptance testing reveals that a Unit does not conform to
Specifications due to Supplier's defect in materials, workmanship, or
manufacturing process, Buyer shall issue a written Defect Notice specifying
the non-conformance. Supplier shall remedy the defect at its own cost and
expense. Upon Supplier's written confirmation that the defect is remedied,
Buyer shall conduct re-acceptance testing within fifteen (15) calendar days.

The **First Correction Period** for a Unit is the number of calendar days
between Buyer's Defect Notice and Supplier's written confirmation of remedy.

The **First Revised Delivery Date** for that Unit is:

> First Revised Delivery Date = Actual Physical Delivery Date + First
> Correction Period

All provisional penalty and incentive amounts for that Unit **shall be
recomputed** substituting the First Revised Delivery Date for the Actual
Physical Delivery Date in Sections 3.1 and 3.2. The recomputed amounts
replace the provisional amounts in their entirety.

### 4.3 Second and Subsequent Defect Corrections

If re-acceptance testing following a First Correction reveals a further defect
(whether the same or a different defect), Buyer may issue a Second Defect
Notice. The process in Section 4.2 repeats. The **Second Correction Period**
is calculated the same way. The **Second Revised Delivery Date** is:

> Second Revised Delivery Date = First Revised Delivery Date + Second
> Correction Period

A third or further revision follows the same pattern: each subsequent Revised
Delivery Date equals the prior Revised Delivery Date plus the next Correction
Period.

### 4.4 Cap on Correction Period Additions

The total calendar days that may be added to a Unit's delivery date across all
Correction Periods combined shall not exceed **60 days per Unit**
(the "Correction Cap"). If the sum of all Correction Periods for a Unit
exceeds 60 days, the final Revised Delivery Date is capped at:

> Capped Revised Delivery Date = Actual Physical Delivery Date + 60 days

Any additional correction time beyond 60 days is still required to be
completed by Supplier (at its expense) but does not further shift the
Revised Delivery Date for penalty/incentive purposes.

### 4.5 Penalty Grace Period Applies to Revised Delivery Date

The 21-day grace period in Section 3.1 applies to the **final Revised Delivery
Date** (after all corrections and the Correction Cap), not to the Actual
Physical Delivery Date. If the final Revised Delivery Date falls within 21
days after the Required Delivery Date, no penalty applies. If it falls beyond
21 days, the full penalty calculation (from day 1, not day 22) applies based
on the revised lateness.

### 4.6 Early Delivery Incentive After Revision

If the final Revised Delivery Date falls **before** the Required Delivery Date,
the early delivery incentive under Section 3.2 is calculated based on the
number of days between the final Revised Delivery Date and the Required
Delivery Date. It is possible for a Unit that was physically delivered early
to lose its incentive (or gain a penalty) after correction periods push its
Revised Delivery Date past the Required Delivery Date.

---

## 5. Holdback

### 5.1 Holdback Amount

Four percent (4%) of the Contract Price (**$240,000**) shall be withheld by
Buyer from the final milestone invoice payment (the "Holdback Amount").

### 5.2 Holdback Release Trigger — Based on ACTUAL Delivery, Not Revised

The Holdback Amount shall be released to Supplier on the date that is
**twelve (12) calendar months after the last Actual Physical Delivery Date**
of any Unit (i.e., the date the last Unit physically arrived at the Austin
Logistics Hub, regardless of any subsequent defect corrections or Revised
Delivery Dates), provided that on such release date all twenty (20) Units are
operating in conformance with their Specifications.

**For the avoidance of doubt:** Correction Periods do not postpone the
Holdback release date. The twelve-month clock runs from Actual Physical
Delivery, not from any Revised Delivery Date.

### 5.3 Pro-Rata Holdback Reduction

If on the Holdback release date fewer than twenty (20) Units are conforming,
the Holdback Amount is reduced by 1/20th ($12,000) per conforming Unit and
that reduced amount is released to Supplier; the remainder is retained by
Buyer as liquidated damages for in-service non-conformance.

---

## 6. Settlement Timing and Final Accounting

### 6.1 No Interim Payments of Penalties or Incentives

No penalty or incentive amounts are paid or withheld during the delivery and
correction process. The full settlement of all penalties and incentives is
deferred until **Final Acceptance**, defined as the date on which all twenty
Units have either been accepted (no defect notice issued within the Testing
Window) or have completed all correction cycles and passed re-acceptance
testing.

### 6.2 Final Accounting Statement

Within thirty (30) days of Final Acceptance, Buyer shall issue a Final
Accounting Statement showing, for each Unit:
- Actual Physical Delivery Date
- Each Correction Period (if any), with dates
- Final Revised Delivery Date (after Correction Cap if applicable)
- Final late delivery penalty or early delivery incentive (recomputed)
- Whether Unit is included in Holdback release calculation

Supplier shall have fifteen (15) days to dispute the Final Accounting
Statement in writing.

## 7. Worked Examples — Contractually Binding

### Example 1 — Single Defect, Incentive Lost

Unit U-03 is physically delivered on PED+195 days (15 days early; provisional
incentive = 15 × $750 = $11,250).

Buyer issues Defect Notice on PED+205. Supplier confirms remedy on PED+225.
First Correction Period = 20 days. First Revised Delivery Date = PED+215.

PED+215 is still before Required Delivery Date (PED+210)? No — PED+215 is 5
days **after** Required Delivery Date. Revised delivery is 5 days late.
Grace period: 5 days ≤ 21 days → **no penalty applies.**
Early delivery incentive: **none** (Revised Delivery Date is after Required
Delivery Date).

Final result for U-03: $0 penalty, $0 incentive. (The $11,250 provisional
incentive is cancelled.)

---

### Example 2 — Two Defects, Cap Applies, Split Reference Point

Unit U-11 is physically delivered on PED+240 days (30 days late, within grace
period initially — let's compute).

30 days > 21-day grace threshold → late penalty applies from day 1.
Provisional penalty = 30 days × $1,500 = $45,000.

Buyer issues First Defect Notice PED+255. Remedy confirmed PED+290.
First Correction Period = 35 days. First Revised Delivery Date = PED+275.
Revised lateness = 275 - 210 = 65 days. Grace threshold: 65 > 21 → full
penalty from day 1. Recomputed penalty = 65 × $1,500 = $97,500.
Exceeds per-Unit cap of $90,000 → **capped at $90,000**.

Buyer issues Second Defect Notice PED+300. Remedy confirmed PED+330.
Second Correction Period = 30 days. Sum of corrections = 35+30 = 65 days.
Correction Cap = 60 days. Capped Revised Delivery Date = PED+240+60 = PED+300.
Revised lateness = 300 - 210 = 90 days. Full penalty from day 1.
Recomputed penalty = 90 × $1,500 = $135,000 → **capped at $90,000**.

**Holdback clock:** Starts from PED+240 (Actual Physical Delivery of U-11,
assuming U-11 is the last Unit delivered). Holdback release date = PED+240+365
= PED+605. Correction Periods do not push this date.

Final result for U-11: $90,000 penalty. Holdback clock runs from PED+240.

---

### Example 3 — The Pathological Case (All Four Clocks Diverge)

Unit U-17 physically delivered PED+195 (15 days early).
Defect found, correction takes 30 days → First Revised Delivery Date = PED+225.
Second defect found, correction takes 40 days → total corrections = 70 days,
Correction Cap = 60 days → Capped Revised Delivery Date = PED+255.

Revised lateness = 255 - 210 = 45 days > 21-day grace → penalty from day 1.
Penalty = 45 × $1,500 = $67,500. Below per-Unit cap. **Penalty = $67,500.**

Early delivery incentive: **none** (Revised Delivery Date is after Required
Delivery Date).

Holdback clock: starts PED+195 (Actual Physical Delivery).
Warranty: 24 months from PED+255 (First/Capped Revised Delivery Date per
Exhibit A warranty clause — note: warranty uses Revised Delivery Date, unlike
Holdback which uses Actual Physical Delivery Date).

Four clocks, four different reference points:
| Clock | Reference Date |
|-------|----------------|
| Late penalty | Capped Revised Delivery Date (PED+255) |
| Early incentive | Capped Revised Delivery Date (PED+255) |
| Holdback | Actual Physical Delivery Date (PED+195) |
| Warranty | Capped Revised Delivery Date (PED+255) |
