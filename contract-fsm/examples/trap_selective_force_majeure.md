# NovaStar–TeleSat Ground Equipment Delivery Agreement
**Contract No. NS-TS-2024-04**
Effective Date: 15 April 2024
Between: NovaStar Systems LLC ("Supplier") and TeleSat Operations Inc. ("Buyer")

---

## 1. Scope

Supplier shall design, manufacture, test, and deliver twelve (12) L-band Ground
Terminal Units ("GTUs") and associated antenna subsystems in accordance with the
Specifications (Exhibit A) and Statement of Work (Exhibit B).

Total fixed price: **$9,600,000** (the "Contract Price").

---

## 2. Delivery Schedule

| Item | Required Delivery Date |
|------|----------------------|
| Engineering Prototype (EP) | AED + 90 days |
| GTUs 1–4 (Batch A) | AED + 270 days |
| GTUs 5–8 (Batch B) | AED + 330 days |
| GTUs 9–12 (Batch C) | AED + 390 days |
| Antenna Subsystems (all) | AED + 420 days |

"AED" means the Agreement Effective Date. Delivery means physical delivery to
Buyer's designated facility in Austin, Texas, confirmed by signed receipt.

## 3. Price Adjustments

### 3.1 Late Delivery Penalties

If any GTU Batch or the Antenna Subsystems is not delivered by its Required
Delivery Date, Supplier shall pay Buyer liquidated damages of **$3,200 per GTU
per calendar day** of delay, beginning on the first day after the applicable
Required Delivery Date. No grace period applies to Batch A. A grace period of
fifteen (15) calendar days applies to Batches B and C and to the Antenna
Subsystems; no penalty accrues within the grace period.

The maximum late delivery penalty shall not exceed **18% of the Contract Price
($1,728,000)** in the aggregate across all Batches and the Antenna Subsystems
combined (the "Aggregate LD Cap"). Daily accrual across all items shall not
exceed **$38,400 per day** in total regardless of how many items are
simultaneously late.

### 3.2 Early Delivery Incentive

If the Engineering Prototype and all GTU Batches (1–12) are delivered no later
than their respective Required Delivery Dates, and any Batch is delivered
**before** its Required Delivery Date, Buyer shall pay Supplier an early
delivery incentive of **$1,600 per GTU per calendar day early**, calculated
using the least-early item within each Batch (i.e., the day-count for a Batch
is controlled by the item in that Batch delivered latest relative to the
Required Delivery Date). The total early delivery incentive shall not exceed
**$180,000** across all Batches combined.

### 3.3 On-Time Completion Bonus

If all twelve (12) GTUs and all Antenna Subsystems are delivered on or before
their respective Required Delivery Dates (after application of Section 3.1
grace periods), Buyer shall pay Supplier an additional one-time on-time
completion bonus of **$115,000**.

## 4. Force Majeure

### 4.1 Definition

Neither party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance caused by
events beyond that party's reasonable control, including but not limited to:
acts of God, war, terrorism, governmental embargo, fire, flood, epidemic,
earthquake, strike or labour dispute not involving that party's own employees,
or failure of a third-party component supplier whose failure could not have
been avoided through commercially reasonable alternate sourcing.

### 4.2 Suspension of Late Delivery Penalties

For each calendar day that a documented force majeure event directly prevents
Supplier from performing the manufacturing, testing, or delivery activities on
the critical path for the affected GTU Batch or Antenna Subsystems, the
accrual of late delivery penalties under Section 3.1 **shall be suspended**
for that Batch or subsystem for that day. Supplier shall provide written notice
within five (5) business days of the onset of a force majeure event and shall
update Buyer weekly with evidence of continued impact.

### 4.3 No Extension of Early Delivery Incentive Window

**Notwithstanding Section 4.2**, the Required Delivery Dates used to calculate
early delivery incentives under Section 3.2 and the on-time completion bonus
under Section 3.3 **shall not be extended or adjusted** by reason of any force
majeure event. The incentive windows remain fixed at the dates set forth in
Section 2 regardless of any force majeure delay. A Batch that is delivered
after its Required Delivery Date due to a force majeure event will not be
eligible for early delivery incentive even if no penalty accrued for the delay.

### 4.4 No Tolling of Holdback Release Clock

The twelve-month Holdback release clock described in Section 6.1 **shall
continue to run without interruption** during any force majeure period.
Force majeure does not pause, extend, or reset the Holdback release date.

### 4.5 No Extension of Warranty Period

The warranty period described in Section 7.1 commences on the actual date of
each GTU's delivery (as determined under Section 5, including any retroactive
adjustment) and **is not extended** by the duration of any force majeure event
that preceded delivery.

## 5. Retroactive Delivery Date Adjustment for Defects

If acceptance testing at Buyer's facility reveals defects in a GTU arising from
Supplier's workmanship, materials, or non-conformance with Specifications,
Supplier shall remedy such defects at its own expense. The elapsed calendar
time between Buyer's written notice of defect and Supplier's written
confirmation of remedy (the "Correction Period") **shall be added to the
actual delivery date** for that GTU to establish a **Revised Delivery Date**
for the purpose of calculating late delivery penalties and early delivery
incentives under Sections 3.1 and 3.2.

*Example:* GTU-3 is physically delivered on AED+280 days (10 days after
Required Delivery Date for Batch A of AED+270). Buyer issues defect notice on
AED+295. Supplier confirms remedy on AED+310. Correction Period = 15 days.
Revised Delivery Date = AED+280+15 = AED+295 days. Late penalty is calculated
as if GTU-3 were delivered AED+295 days (25 days late), not 10 days late.

The Correction Period used for adjusting incentive calculations (Section 3.2)
shall be **the same Revised Delivery Date** as used for penalty calculations.
No separate adjustment applies for incentives.

## 6. Holdback

### 6.1 Holdback Amount and Conditions

Five percent (5%) of the Contract Price (**$480,000**) (the "Holdback Amount")
shall be withheld by Buyer from the final milestone payment. The Holdback
Amount shall be released to Supplier on the date that is twelve (12) calendar
months after **the later of** (a) the delivery of the last GTU (using Revised
Delivery Dates per Section 5 where applicable) and (b) the delivery of the
Antenna Subsystems, provided that on such date all twelve GTUs are operating
in conformance with their Specifications.

If on the twelve-month anniversary one or more GTUs are not so conforming,
the Holdback Amount shall be reduced pro rata (by 1/12th per non-conforming
GTU) and the reduced amount released to Supplier; the remainder shall be
retained by Buyer as liquidated damages for in-orbit non-conformance.

### 6.2 Force Majeure Does Not Toll Holdback Clock

For the avoidance of doubt, the twelve-month Holdback release clock described
in Section 6.1 begins on the date determined under Section 6.1(a)/(b) and
**is not suspended, extended, or reset** by any force majeure event occurring
after that date.

---

## 7. Warranty

### 7.1 Duration

Supplier warrants each GTU against defects in materials and workmanship for a
period of **twenty-four (24) months** from the Revised Delivery Date of that
GTU (as determined under Section 5). Force majeure events occurring prior to
delivery do not extend the warranty period.

## 8. Illustrations of Correct Calculation

The following examples are contractually binding as interpretive guidance.

**Scenario A — Force Majeure, No Incentive:**
A fire at Supplier's subcontractor causes a 45-day force majeure delay to
Batch B. Batch B is delivered 40 days after its Required Delivery Date.
- Late penalties: suspended for 45 days, zero penalty owed (delay < FM period)
- Early delivery incentive: **not available** — Required Delivery Date is not
  extended; Batch B was delivered after its fixed Required Delivery Date
- On-time completion bonus: **not available** — Batch B missed its date
- Holdback clock: **not affected** — runs from actual last delivery date

**Scenario B — Defect Correction Crosses Force Majeure Boundary:**
GTU-7 is delivered on AED+325 (within Batch B grace period, no penalty).
Defect notice issued AED+335; during remedy period (AED+335 to AED+360) a
force majeure event also occurs (AED+340 to AED+355). Remedy confirmed
AED+360. Correction Period = 25 days. Revised Delivery Date = AED+350.
- Late penalty: AED+350 vs Required Date AED+330 = 5 days outside grace,
  penalty on 5 days. Force majeure during correction period does **not**
  reduce the Correction Period added to the delivery date.
- Holdback clock: starts from AED+360 (last actual delivery of last item).
