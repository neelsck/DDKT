# Meridian–ClearPath Infrastructure Monitoring System Delivery Agreement
**Contract No. MC-CP-2024-07**
Effective Date: 1 July 2024
Between: Meridian Engineering Group Inc. ("Supplier") and ClearPath Municipal
Infrastructure Authority ("Buyer")

---

## 1. Scope and Price

Supplier shall design, manufacture, integrate, test, and commission an
automated Infrastructure Monitoring System ("IMS") for Buyer's fourteen (14)
regional water-distribution monitoring nodes, including sensors, communication
hardware, central software platform, and staff training, in accordance with the
Statement of Work (Exhibit A) and Technical Specifications (Exhibit B).

Total fixed price: **$5,400,000** (the "Contract Price").

---

## 2. Delivery and Acceptance Schedule

| Milestone | Description | Due Date |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| PDR | Preliminary Design Review completed | CED + 45 days |
| CDR | Critical Design Review completed | CED + 90 days |
| FAT | Factory Acceptance Testing passed | CED + 180 days |
| DEL | System physically delivered to Buyer's Operations Center | CED + 210 days |
| SAT | Site Acceptance Testing completed | CED + 240 days |
| COM | System commissioned and live at all 14 nodes | CED + 270 days |

"CED" means Contract Effective Date. Each milestone is complete upon Buyer's
written acceptance notice. "Required Delivery Date" refers to the DEL milestone
date (CED + 210 days).

---

## 3. Late Delivery Penalties

### 3.1 Daily Accrual

If the DEL milestone is not completed by the Required Delivery Date, Supplier
shall pay Buyer liquidated damages of **$4,500 per calendar day** of delay,
beginning on the first day after the Required Delivery Date.

A grace period of **fourteen (14) calendar days** applies; no penalty accrues
within the grace period. If delay exceeds 14 days, all days from day 1 are
penalised retroactively (threshold trigger, not an offset).

### 3.2 Penalty Cap

Late delivery penalties shall not exceed **$405,000** (7.5% of Contract Price).

### 3.3 Milestone Credit

If any of PDR, CDR, or FAT is completed ahead of schedule, the number of
calendar days of early completion shall be credited against late delivery
penalties calculated under Section 3.1, up to a maximum credit of **10 days**
in aggregate across all three pre-delivery milestones.

---

## 4. Early Delivery Incentive

If the DEL milestone is completed before the Required Delivery Date, Buyer
shall pay Supplier **$2,250 per calendar day early**, calculated from the
actual delivery date to the Required Delivery Date. The early delivery
incentive shall not exceed **$63,000** (the "Early Delivery Cap").

---

## 5. Site Acceptance Testing

### 5.1 Testing Period

Following physical delivery (DEL), Buyer shall conduct Site Acceptance Testing
within thirty (30) calendar days. If Buyer does not issue a Defect Notice within
this period, SAT is deemed passed and the Agreement proceeds to commissioning.

### 5.2 Defect Correction

If SAT reveals defects, Supplier shall remedy at its own expense. The elapsed
time between Buyer's written Defect Notice and Supplier's written confirmation
of remedy is the "Correction Period." The Revised Delivery Date for penalty
and incentive purposes equals the Actual DEL Date plus the Correction Period,
subject to a maximum addition of **45 days** across all correction rounds.

### 5.3 Acceptance

Upon SAT passage (original or after correction), Buyer issues a written
Acceptance Certificate. The contract transitions to Warranty Active status.

---

## 6. Holdback

### 6.1 Holdback Amount

Ten percent (10%) of the Contract Price (**$540,000**) shall be withheld by
Buyer from the final payment milestone invoice (the "Holdback Amount").

### 6.2 Holdback Release Conditions

The Holdback Amount shall be released to Supplier in full within thirty (30)
calendar days of the later of:

(a) twelve (12) calendar months after the COM milestone acceptance date; and

(b) resolution of all open warranty claims lodged before the twelve-month
anniversary of COM.

Partial release is not available; the full Holdback Amount is either released
or retained (subject to deduction under Section 6.3).

### 6.3 Holdback Deduction for Warranty Non-Conformance

If, on the Holdback release date, one or more of the fourteen monitoring nodes
is not operating in conformance with the Specifications, Buyer may deduct from
the Holdback Amount **$38,571 per non-conforming node** (equal to 1/14th of the
Holdback Amount) and release the remainder to Supplier. Retained amounts are
liquidated damages for in-service non-conformance; no further warranty claim
may be brought in respect of those nodes.

### 6.4 Holdback Dispute Procedure

**[NOTE: This Section is superseded by Section 14.3; see Section 14.3.]**

If Supplier disputes a Holdback deduction made by Buyer under Section 6.3,
Supplier may, within fifteen (15) calendar days of Buyer's written deduction
notice, initiate the Holdback Dispute procedure by delivering a written
Dispute Notice to Buyer specifying the grounds for dispute. Upon delivery of a
valid Dispute Notice, the contract shall enter **Holdback Dispute status** for
a period of sixty (60) calendar days (the "Dispute Resolution Window"). During
this period:

(i)   The disputed deduction amount shall be placed in a segregated escrow
      account maintained by a mutually agreed escrow agent;

(ii)  Buyer and Supplier shall designate one technical representative each to
      form a Joint Resolution Panel, which shall meet within fifteen (15)
      calendar days of Dispute Notice;

(iii) The Joint Resolution Panel shall inspect the non-conforming nodes and
      issue a written finding within thirty (30) calendar days;

(iv)  If the Panel's finding is accepted by both parties, the escrow shall be
      distributed accordingly and Holdback Dispute status shall resolve;

(v)   If the Panel's finding is not accepted by either party within fifteen (15)
      calendar days, the dispute shall escalate to binding arbitration under
      Section 14.

The Holdback Dispute status terminates upon: (A) mutual acceptance of the
Joint Resolution Panel finding, (B) commencement of arbitration proceedings
under Section 14, or (C) expiration of the Dispute Resolution Window without
escalation, in which case the deduction stands and Supplier's right to
challenge that deduction is waived.

## 7. Warranty

### 7.1 Duration and Scope

Supplier warrants the IMS against defects in materials, workmanship, and
conformance to Specifications for **twenty-four (24) calendar months** from the
COM acceptance date. This warranty covers all fourteen monitoring nodes,
communication hardware, and central software.

### 7.2 Warranty Claims

Buyer shall submit warranty claims in writing, specifying the non-conformance
and the affected node(s). Supplier shall remedy conforming warranty claims
within **thirty (30) calendar days** of written notice at no cost to Buyer.
Failure to remedy within this period entitles Buyer to procure third-party
remediation and invoice Supplier for reasonable costs, up to $25,000 per
incident.

---

## 8. Force Majeure

### 8.1 Definition and Scope

Neither party shall be liable for delay or non-performance caused by events
beyond that party's reasonable control, including acts of God, war,
governmental embargo, flood, earthquake, or failure of a third-party
sub-supplier whose failure could not have been avoided through commercially
reasonable alternate sourcing.

### 8.2 Effect on Penalty Accrual

Late delivery penalties under Section 3.1 shall be suspended for each calendar
day that a documented force majeure event directly prevents Supplier from
performing critical-path activities. Supplier shall provide written notice
within five (5) business days of onset and weekly updates thereafter.

### 8.3 No Effect on Incentive Window

The Required Delivery Date used to calculate early delivery incentives under
Section 4 shall not be adjusted for force majeure. Incentive windows are fixed.

### 8.4 No Effect on Holdback Clock

The Holdback release timeline in Section 6.2 is not suspended or extended by
force majeure.

incidental, or consequential damages.

## 14. Dispute Resolution

### 14.1 Mandatory Binding Arbitration

**Any and all disputes, claims, or controversies arising out of or relating to
this Agreement**, including disputes concerning its formation, performance,
breach, termination, or validity, shall be resolved exclusively by binding
arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") under
its Commercial Arbitration Rules, as modified by this Section 14. The
arbitration shall be conducted in Austin, Texas, before a single arbitrator
selected from the AAA's Large Complex Case roster.

### 14.2 Arbitration as Sole Remedy

The arbitration procedure in this Section 14 is the **sole and exclusive
dispute resolution mechanism** for this Agreement. No party shall initiate
litigation or any other dispute resolution procedure with respect to any
dispute that is or could be subject to this Section 14, except to seek
interim injunctive relief pending arbitration.

### 14.3 Holdback Disputes — Direct Arbitration; Section 6.4 Superseded

**Notwithstanding Section 6.4**, any dispute by Supplier regarding a Holdback
deduction made by Buyer under Section 6.3 shall be submitted **directly to
arbitration** under Section 14.1. The Holdback Dispute procedure described in
Section 6.4 — including the Dispute Notice requirement, Holdback Dispute
status, and the Joint Resolution Panel — **is superseded in its entirety** by
this Section 14.3 and shall have no force or effect. Supplier's sole remedy
upon disagreeing with a Holdback deduction is to file for arbitration under
Section 14.1 within thirty (30) calendar days of Buyer's written deduction
notice; failure to do so within this period is a waiver of Supplier's right to
contest the deduction.

The escrow mechanism described in Section 6.4(i) is preserved: pending
resolution of any arbitration proceeding initiated under this Section 14.3,
the disputed deduction amount shall be held in escrow on the same terms as
described in Section 6.4(i).

### 14.4 Arbitration Fees

The costs of arbitration, including arbitrator fees, shall be borne equally by
the parties, except that the prevailing party may seek recovery of reasonable
attorneys' fees and arbitration costs from the non-prevailing party as part of
the arbitral award.

## 15. Worked Examples — Contractually Binding

### Example A — On-Time Delivery, Clean Acceptance

DEL occurs on CED+210 (Required Delivery Date). No defects found in SAT.
COM accepted on CED+268 (2 days early vs. schedule).

- Late delivery penalty: $0 (no delay)
- Early delivery incentive: $0 (DEL was not early — incentive is per DEL date)
- Holdback: $540,000 withheld at COM acceptance.
- Holdback release date: CED+268+365 = CED+633, assuming all 14 nodes conforming.
- Holdback released in full: $540,000 to Supplier.

### Example B — Late Delivery, Two Nodes Non-Conforming at Holdback Release

DEL occurs on CED+228 (18 days late, exceeds 14-day grace).
No milestone credits apply.

Late penalty: 18 days × $4,500 = $81,000. Below cap.

SAT: passed with no defects. COM: CED+285.
Holdback release date: CED+285+365 = CED+650.

At CED+650: nodes N-03 and N-11 are non-conforming.
Holdback deduction: 2 × $38,571 = $77,142.
Holdback released: $540,000 − $77,142 = **$462,858** to Supplier.
$77,142 retained by Buyer as liquidated damages for nodes N-03 and N-11.

Supplier disputes the N-03 deduction ($38,571). Under Section 14.3, Supplier
must file for AAA arbitration within 30 calendar days of Buyer's deduction
notice. The $38,571 goes to escrow pending arbitration. Section 6.4's Joint
Resolution Panel procedure **does not apply**.

### Example C — Force Majeure Mid-Delivery

A flood event suspends Supplier's manufacturing for 22 days. DEL occurs
CED+230 (20 days after Required Delivery Date, before force majeure
adjustment). Supplier documents 22 days of force majeure.

Late penalty accrual: suspended for 22 days. Since the total delay (20 days)
is fully within the 22-day force majeure period, **no penalty applies**.

Early delivery incentive: Required Delivery Date not extended by force majeure
(Section 8.3). DEL at CED+230 is after CED+210. **No incentive applies.**

Holdback clock: not extended by force majeure (Section 8.4). Runs from
COM acceptance date as normal.
