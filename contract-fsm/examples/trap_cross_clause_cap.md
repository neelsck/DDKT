# CivicBridge–Dataline Municipal Software Delivery Agreement
**Contract No. CB-DL-2023-11**
Effective Date: 1 November 2023
Between: CivicBridge Technologies Inc. ("Vendor") and Dataline Municipal
Services Authority ("Authority")

---

## 1. Scope and Price

Vendor shall deliver, configure, and commission an integrated permitting and
inspections software platform (the "Platform") for Authority's seven (7)
municipal districts, including all hardware components, staff training, and
documentation, in accordance with the Statement of Work (Exhibit A).

Total fixed price: **$4,200,000** (the "Contract Price").

---

## 2. Delivery Milestones

| Milestone | Description | Due Date |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| M1 | Software core delivered to test environment | CED + 60 days |
| M2 | Hardware units delivered to all 7 districts | CED + 90 days |
| M3 | Training completed for all district staff | CED + 120 days |
| M4 | Platform live in all 7 districts (Go-Live) | CED + 150 days |
| M5 | Documentation package delivered | CED + 165 days |

"CED" means the Contract Effective Date. Each milestone is considered complete
when Authority's Project Manager issues written milestone acceptance.

---

## 3. Liquidated Damages — Three Penalty Streams

The parties acknowledge that late or deficient performance under this contract
will cause Authority quantifiable harm and that the following liquidated
damages represent a reasonable pre-estimate of such harm.

### 3.1 Stream A — Software Delivery Delay (Per Milestone)

For each calendar day that Milestone M1 or Milestone M4 (Go-Live) remains
incomplete past its Due Date, Vendor shall pay Authority **$2,500 per day**
per overdue milestone (i.e., up to $5,000/day if both M1 and M4 are
simultaneously overdue).

Stream A individual cap: **$375,000** (i.e., Stream A penalties shall not
exceed $375,000 in total regardless of duration or number of overdue
milestones).

### 3.2 Stream B — Hardware Non-Delivery (Per District Unit)

For each calendar day that hardware for a district remains undelivered past
the M2 Due Date, Vendor shall pay Authority **$800 per district per day**.
With seven districts, the maximum daily accrual under Stream B is $5,600/day.

Stream B individual cap: **$280,000** (i.e., Stream B penalties shall not
exceed $280,000 in total regardless of how many districts are affected or for
how long).

### 3.3 Stream C — Training and Documentation Delay

For each calendar day that Milestone M3 (Training) remains incomplete past its
Due Date, Vendor shall pay Authority **$1,200 per day**. For each calendar day
that Milestone M5 (Documentation) remains incomplete past its Due Date, Vendor
shall pay Authority **$600 per day**. M3 and M5 accrue independently but both
count toward Stream C.

Stream C individual cap: **$145,000** (i.e., Stream C penalties — from M3 and
M5 combined — shall not exceed $145,000 in total).

---

## 4. Aggregate Liquidated Damages Cap

**Notwithstanding the individual stream caps in Sections 3.1, 3.2, and 3.3,**
the total liquidated damages payable by Vendor to Authority under this
Agreement from all streams combined (Stream A + Stream B + Stream C) shall not
exceed **$600,000** (the "Aggregate Cap").

The Aggregate Cap is an absolute ceiling. Once the running total of accrued
penalties across all three streams reaches $600,000, accrual under **all three
streams ceases simultaneously**, regardless of whether any individual stream
has reached its own cap.

### 4.1 Pro-Rata Allocation at the Aggregate Cap

If accrual under all three streams is ongoing when the Aggregate Cap is
reached, and the remaining headroom before the cap is less than the combined
daily accrual rate, the final partial day's accrual shall be allocated among
the streams **pro rata by each stream's total accrued amount at that moment**.

*Example:* At the moment the Aggregate Cap is about to be reached, Stream A
has accrued $280,000, Stream B has accrued $220,000, and Stream C has accrued
$88,000. Combined = $588,000. Remaining headroom = $12,000. The $12,000 is
allocated: Stream A receives $12,000 × (280/588) = $5,714; Stream B receives
$12,000 × (220/588) = $4,490; Stream C receives $12,000 × (88/588) = $1,796.
All streams then cease.

### 4.2 Individual Cap Still Applies Within Aggregate

The individual stream caps in Sections 3.1–3.3 continue to apply within the
Aggregate Cap. A stream that reaches its individual cap stops accruing for
that stream even if the Aggregate Cap has not been reached. The remaining
streams continue to accrue (subject to the Aggregate Cap).

*Example of sequential capping:* Stream C reaches its $145,000 individual cap
first. Stream C stops. Streams A and B continue accruing, subject to a
combined remaining headroom of $600,000 − (Stream A accrued) − (Stream B
accrued) − $145,000.

---

## 5. Partial Credit for Partial Performance

### 5.1 Partial Hardware Delivery

For Stream B, if hardware is delivered to some but not all districts by the
M2 Due Date, daily penalties accrue only for districts where hardware remains
undelivered. A district is considered delivered when hardware is received and
signed for by that district's designated contact. Partial delivery to a
district (e.g., 3 of 5 required units) does not satisfy the district's
delivery requirement; the full $800/day applies until all units for that
district are delivered.

### 5.2 Partial Training Completion

For Stream C (M3 component), if training is completed for some but not all
district staff cohorts by the M3 Due Date, the $1,200/day accrual is reduced
by $171.43 (= $1,200 ÷ 7) for each district whose staff training is completed,
and continues at the reduced rate for remaining uncompleted districts.

---

## 6. Incentive Payment

If all five milestones are accepted by Authority on or before their respective
Due Dates, Authority shall pay Vendor an on-time completion incentive of
**$84,000** (2% of Contract Price). No partial incentive is available; all
five milestones must be accepted on time for the incentive to be earned.

The on-time completion incentive is independent of the liquidated damages
calculation. Vendor may earn the incentive and owe zero liquidated damages, or
owe liquidated damages and receive no incentive, but the two amounts are not
netted against each other; they are tracked and settled separately.

## 7. Worked Example — Correct Aggregate Calculation

The following example is contractually binding as interpretive guidance.

**Facts:**
- M1 is 40 days late. M4 is 60 days late.
- Hardware delivered to 3 districts on time; 4 districts are 55 days late.
- M3 (Training) is 70 days late.
- M5 (Documentation) is delivered on time.

**Naive (wrong) calculation:**
- Stream A: 40 days × $2,500 (M1) + 60 days × $2,500 (M4) = $250,000
  → below Stream A cap of $375,000 → Stream A = $250,000
- Stream B: 4 districts × 55 days × $800 = $176,000
  → below Stream B cap of $280,000 → Stream B = $176,000
- Stream C: 70 days × $1,200 = $84,000
  → below Stream C cap of $145,000 → Stream C = $84,000
- Naive total: $250,000 + $176,000 + $84,000 = **$510,000**
  → naive answer says $510,000 — appears fine, under Aggregate Cap

*(In this example the Aggregate Cap doesn't bind, so naive is correct.
The trap fires when individual streams are larger — see below.)*

**Trap scenario:**
- M1 is 80 days late. M4 is 90 days late. (Stream A: 80×$2,500 + 90×$2,500 =
  $425,000 → **capped at $375,000**)
- All 7 districts 90 days late. (Stream B: 7×90×$800 = $504,000 →
  **capped at $280,000**)
- M3 is 90 days late, M5 is 60 days late.
  (Stream C: 90×$1,200 + 60×$600 = $108,000+$36,000 = $144,000 →
  below $145,000 cap → Stream C = $144,000)

**Naive (wrong) answer:** $375,000 + $280,000 + $144,000 = **$799,000**
→ naive system applies individual caps correctly but ignores Aggregate Cap

**Correct answer:** $375,000 + $280,000 + $144,000 = $799,000 pre-cap
→ **Aggregate Cap of $600,000 applies** → total LD = **$600,000**

Allocation of the $600,000 among streams (pro-rata by accrued pre-cap amounts,
for record-keeping and audit purposes only — does not affect total owed):
- Stream A share: $600,000 × (375/799) = $281,602
- Stream B share: $600,000 × (280/799) = $210,263
- Stream C share: $600,000 × (144/799) = $108,135
